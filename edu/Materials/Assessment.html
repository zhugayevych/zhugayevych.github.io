Assessment criteria (what is considered as A, see also grading scheme):

  • Homework: All tasks are completed accurately and in full, without redundancy.
  • Presentation: Scientific presentation style, content is delivered in full, without redundancy, slides are well organized and properly formatted.
  • Report: Scientific article style, content is delivered in full, without redundancy, conclusions are fully supported.
  • Research: Scholarly quality of your work including accurate and supported statements, knowledge of the subject, presented computed and measured data and facts are analyzed and rationalized.
  • Final project discussions: Ask 3 good questions/comments, bad questions/comments are counted with minus sign, clarifying questions are not counted.
  • Scientific communication skills: Clearly communicate your thoughts and answer questions using scientific language during presentations and discussions.
  • Participation: Active participation in all class activities (assessment period is not counted).

Assessment rules:

  • Become familiar with Skoltech regulations on grading, academic integrity, and attendance.
  • For written submissions you will get written comments: every critical comment decreases your grade by one level in the row A/B/C/D/F.
  • For homeworks if, let say, 8 completed tasks correspond to A, then B corresponds to 6.5 completed tasks. If majority of students failed on a given task, that task is excluded from downgrading.
  • Identical homework solutions are not counted.
  • Additional homework tasks may be taken during assessment week to improve grades (C and lower): submit a single request for all tasks by email or via shared Google sheet. The deadline is 1 day after you obtained your last homework evaluated. Then all requests are processed and extra homework is uploaded here.
  • Team projects can be resubmitted by email two times.
  • Do not forget that any resubmissions or extra tasks can not only increase but also decrease your grade.
  • Final projects are assessed as follows:
    • 40% – written report (75% content, 25% presentation)
    • 40% – oral presentation (50% content, 25% presentation, 25% discussion)
    • 20% – discussion of other projects

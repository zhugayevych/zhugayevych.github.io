Practice

[Aksenov] Modeling of point defects in Li:

  1. Determine equilibrium lattice parameter of bcc Li using conventional cubic cell with two atoms (recitationCD/Li2.full_relax).

  2. Create 3x3x3 supercell of Li and calculate total energy (recitationCD/Li333.opt). Compare energies per atom with primitive cell. Why they are slightly different?

  3. Create vacancy, calculate total energy. Optimize the structure and calculate relaxation energy (recitationCD/Li333vac.opt). Calculate vacancy formation energy and compare it with relaxation energy.

  4. Add self-interstitial atom into tetrahedral and octahedral voids (InterstitialBCC.jpg). Relax the structure, calculate total energy and formation energies (recitationCD/Li333tet.opt, recitationCD/Li333oct.opt). Explain why formation energies are different. Compare relaxation energies of vacancy and self-interstitial.

  5. Go to 4x4x4 or even 5x5x5 supercell to check the influence of supercell size on the formation energy of interstitial defects.

  6. Estimate equilibrium concentrations of vacancies and self-interstitials at room temperature.

  7. Create 3x1x1 supercell of Li with (100) surface. Why do we need to increase the cell size only in [100] direction?

  8. Vary the thickness of vacuum layer from 1 to 10 A and for each case optimize the atomic positions and calculate total energies (recitationCD/Li_suf_i.opt). Calculate surface energies (in J/m2) and plot them against vacuum thickness (gamma.eps). Determine the optimal vacuum thickness.

  9. Compare calculated formation energies of defects with that in literature (literature/*).

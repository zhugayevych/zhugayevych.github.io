Description: The course provides an introduction to physics and chemistry of organic materials as well as an overview of their applications in optoelectronics and energy conversion and storage with a focus on the development of practical skills in materials design. Students will learn about pi-conjugated semiconductors from molecules and molecular solids to polymers and materials with 2D and 3D connectivity such as graphene and metal-organic frameworks. Students will get basic skills in the design of organic field-effect transistors, solar cells, rechargeable batteries.

Intended learning outcomes:

  • Knowledge of physico-chemical properties of organic materials and understanding of relationships between properties of these materials and their functional characteristics in energy and optoelectronic applications.
  • Ability to perform a review of available scientific and technological data and generate structure-property relationships with respect to specific research or engineering task.
  • Skills to perform rational design of materials with predefined physico-chemical and functional characteristics.
  • Experience in processing, characterization, and modeling of organic materials and devices based on them.
  • Ability to communicate research results orally and through writing.

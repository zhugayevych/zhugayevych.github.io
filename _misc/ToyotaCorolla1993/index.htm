Warm-up (thermostat) tips

  • Temperature indicator must be exactly in the middle for the warmed-up engine. Warm-up time must be no more than 5 minutes at 10Â°C. If not, replace the thermostat.
  • Cold engine has 1500 RPM at parking gear, warmed-up engine must have 1000 RPM or less.
  • Cold transmission can not be set to overdrive (4th) gear, until the engine temperature indicator reaches the middle position between zero and first nonzero tickmarks (this is normal). Once 4th gear is set its subsequent engaging does not influenced by the temperature.

Multiple gear ratios for the overdrive

The overdrive (4th) gear actually works at two gear ratios (GR) as detected by RPM: at 60 mph engine shows 3000 RPM at 3rd gear (GR 1.3), 2100 RPM at normal 4th gear (GR 0.9), and 2300-2500 RPM at undocumented overdrive gear (GR 1.0-1.1). Shifting between 3th and normal 4th gears occurs through the undocumented gear. Normal 4th gear is engaged only if the engine temperature indicator is above the first nonzero position, otherwise the undocumented gear is the highest one.
